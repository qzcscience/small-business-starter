Leak testing is a quality control step for products that must hold pressure, maintain vacuum, protect internal components, or prevent fluid and gas escape. Among available methods, helium mass spectrometry remains one of the most sensitive and widely used options for demanding industrial applications.

Helium is useful as a tracer gas because it is inert, non-toxic, and naturally present in air only at low concentration. Its small atomic size helps it pass through very small leak paths. A helium mass spectrometer leak detector can then identify the escaping helium and display a quantitative leak rate.

WAYEAL's public SFJ-231 product information lists both vacuum and sniffer testing modes, with a color touch screen interface, numerical and graphical leak rate display, and communication interfaces for production use. The same information describes application areas including lithium battery manufacturing, cooling systems, battery packs, and other industrial quality assurance tasks.

The choice between vacuum mode, sniffer mode, and chamber-based testing depends on the part and the process. Vacuum testing is often used when higher sensitivity is needed and the test condition can be controlled. Sniffer testing can be useful for larger parts or localized inspection points.

For production teams, helium leak testing is most effective when the method, fixture, detector performance, calibration routine, and acceptance criteria are designed together. That is how a sensitive measurement becomes a stable manufacturing control step.
