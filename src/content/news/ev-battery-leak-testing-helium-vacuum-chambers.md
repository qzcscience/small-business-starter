New energy manufacturing places strict demands on sealing quality. Battery cells, modules, packs, and cooling components must be checked for leaks that could affect safety, service life, and production yield. WAYEAL's public SFJ-231 helium leak detector information describes helium mass spectrometry as a high-sensitivity method for identifying leaks in lithium-ion battery applications.

In battery cell production, helium leak testing can be used to verify sealing after filling and closing processes. For modules and battery packs, testing may focus on enclosures, seams, connectors, and areas where environmental protection is required. For thermal management components, helium mass spectrometry can help confirm the integrity of cooling channels and plates before the parts move downstream.

Vacuum chamber testing is especially useful when parts need a controlled test environment and high sensitivity. In a typical chamber method, the test object is placed in a vacuum chamber and helium escaping from the part is detected by the mass spectrometer. For larger assemblies or local inspection points, sniffer testing may be more practical.

WAYEAL's leak testing portfolio includes helium leak detectors and vacuum chamber leak detection systems, allowing engineering teams to match the method to the part geometry, process stage, and production takt time. That method selection is critical because battery applications can involve both small sealed cells and larger pack-level assemblies.

The goal is not only to find leaks. It is to build a repeatable quality control step that supports stable production and clear pass-fail decisions.
